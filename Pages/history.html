History of Caldes d'Estrac

Explore the rich history, beautiful landscapes, and must-visit locations.

The towns origins dates to the 13th century when an ancient hospital as well as a church were built. Between the 1870s and the 1920s Caldes d’Estrac quickly grew due to its fashionable spa destination. This attracted Barcelona’s bourgeoisie to come and enjoy their therapeutic properties the town had to offer. It attracted many visitors who where looking for relaxation thanks to its thermal baths and its beaches.

Caldetas has also been the home of numerous famous Catalan poets. Writers such as Apel·les Mestres, Joan Maragall, Jacint Verdaguer, and Josep Palau i Fabre have enriched the town and its cultural heritage.

Durning the Spanish Civil War the town Caldes d’Estrac was used as a refuge for all of the embassies that were housed in the city of Barcelona in order to protect them from the bombings. This pinpointed Caldetas as a town with strategic and diplomatic importance.

The Santa Maria Parish Church was constructed between 1805 and 1829 atop a 16th-century church. Its architecture is characterized by elements like the baptismal font and Gothic capitals.

The town also contains two defense towers originating from the 16th century. They are known as La Torre Verda and La Torre Busquets.

In 2003 Caldetas inaugurated the Fundació Palau which is an art center that showcases the personal collection of Josep Palau i Fabre. Within it there a few significant works by Pablo Picasso.
